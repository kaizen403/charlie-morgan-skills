# Building Outbound Systems

> "If you can't get clients right now and you're struggling to book appointments, the chances are it's your foundations, your mindset, your service, or your sales process — not your cold email script. Don't shiny-object your way here. Outbound only works on top of solid foundations."

## When to use

Load this skill any time a task involves:
- Standing up a cold outreach system from scratch
- Diagnosing an underperforming outbound channel
- Choosing tools (scheduler, CRM, lead source, sending software)
- Sequencing channels (where to start, what to add second, what to add third)
- Auditing whether someone "doing outbound" actually has a system or just activity
- Coaching a beginner who skipped weeks 1–4 and jumped straight to "give me the script"

This is the operations-layer companion to `asymmetric-psychological-leverage` (which handles the *content* of stimuli) and `outbound-inbound-rulebook` (which handles when to use outbound vs inbound).

## The shiny-object warning

Outbound systems are the most-skipped-to topic in the entire curriculum because new entrepreneurs believe their problem is "I just need a script." It almost never is.

If outbound isn't producing calls, the cause is *almost always* one of:
1. **Niche** is wrong / too broad / not buying right now → fix with `niche-selection-grade-a`.
2. **Identity / mindset** isn't congruent with someone who deserves and will defend $3K+ pricing → fix with `identity-ignition`, `subjugating-reality`.
3. **Offer** is weak (no result clarity, no risk reversal, no specificity) → fix with `offer-architecture`.
4. **Sales process** can't close calls when they happen → fix with `imperium-conversion-playbook`, `sales-bedrock`.

A perfect outbound system on top of any one of these defects produces nothing. Audit those four before tweaking outbound.

## What an outbound system actually is

The phrase "outbound system" is an aggregate term. A car is wheels + chassis + engine + windscreen + seats. An outbound system is **seven components** assembled together. Without all seven, you do not have a system — you have outbound *activity*, which is fundamentally different and produces inconsistent results.

### The 7 components

1. **Scheduler** — software with a configured event so prospects can book a call. (Recommended: cal.com on the team plan to remove branding.)
2. **Platform** — the channel you reach out on (cold email, social DM, phone, postal mail).
3. **Stimuli** — the persuasive content exposed to prospects (subject line, body copy, voicemail script, opener, follow-up GIF, etc.). Quality of stimuli is governed by `asymmetric-psychological-leverage`.
4. **CRM sheet** — a spreadsheet (or proper CRM) tracking every prospect through the pipeline: contacted, replied, booked, showed, closed.
5. **Lead source** — where you generate prospects' contact info (Apollo, Sales Navigator, scraped lists, manual research, agencies, niche-specific databases).
6. **Metric tracker** — separate spreadsheet tracking activity-level data: messages sent, open rate, reply rate, positive reply rate, booked rate, show rate, close rate. Without metrics you cannot tune.
7. **Delivery mechanism** — the software that actually sends the stimuli (Instantly / Smartlead for email; manual or SOPs for DMs; Aircall / phone / dialer for cold calls).

If any one of the seven is missing, you don't have a system. Build them in order.

## Step-by-step build order

### Step 1 — Set up your scheduler
Sign up for cal.com using the Google Workspace email you set up in week 1. Connect the Google Calendar. Connect Zoom. Set availability *in Google Calendar* (block off non-call hours) and leave cal.com 24/7 — that way you only manage availability in one place. Add a professional headshot. Skip the "About" — fancy bios kill conversions. Upgrade to the team plan annually so you can remove branding and unlock the features you'll need at volume.

### Step 2 — Choose your platform(s)
The four real channels:
- **Cold email** — highest volume, lowest cost per touch, longest learning curve on deliverability. Best as a primary at 1,000–10,000+ touches/day potential.
- **Social DM** (Instagram, LinkedIn, X, Facebook) — medium volume, higher engagement when matched to niche, easier for beginners.
- **Cold calling** — lowest volume per hour, highest conversion per touch, brutal mental load. Excellent as a secondary or tertiary channel for high-ticket niches.
- **Postal/direct mail** — niche, used at scale by sophisticated operators for top-tier ICPs ($50K+ deals).

Default sequencing for a beginner: **Primary = cold email** (volume), **Secondary = social DMs** (relationship building), **Tertiary = cold calling** (closer-only signal). Cold calling priority depends on whether your niche is reachable by phone. (Some aren't — e.g., busy lawyers, executives behind gatekeepers.)

### Step 3 — Build the stimuli library
For each channel, you need:
- Opening message variants (3–10)
- Follow-up variants (4–7 per sequence)
- Subject lines (10+ tested)
- Calendar-link CTA wording
- Voicemail script (if calling)
- Reply handling templates (interested / not interested / wrong person / not now)

Use `asymmetric-psychological-leverage` to design each stimulus to fire 5–10 cognitive bias pins. Polarized > beige.

### Step 4 — Build the CRM sheet
Columns at minimum:
`Date Added | Name | Title | Company | Email | Phone | Channel | Last Touch Date | Last Touch Type | Status | Booked Date | Showed | Closed | Revenue | Notes`

Status values: `Cold | Contacted | Opened | Replied (positive) | Replied (negative) | Booked | No-show | Showed | Closed | Lost`

### Step 5 — Set up lead sourcing
Pick *one* lead source and master it. Common starting stack:
- **Apollo.io** — broad B2B, good for email enrichment and filters.
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** — deeper firmographic and titles, manual workflow.
- **Niche-specific databases** — e.g., for real-estate agents, gym owners, coaches, the database depends entirely on the niche. Find it via niche immersion.

Build a list of 500–2,000 prospects matching your ICP before turning on outreach. Lists smaller than ~500 don't produce statistically meaningful feedback.

### Step 6 — Build the metric tracker
Spreadsheet with daily/weekly rows. Columns:
`Date | Messages Sent | Opens | Open % | Replies | Reply % | Positive Replies | Pos Reply % | Calls Booked | Booked % | Showed | Show % | Closed | Close %`

You read this dashboard weekly. It is the *only* honest source of feedback. Subjective sense ("I think outbound is working") is worthless. The metrics are the oracle.

### Step 7 — Stand up the delivery mechanism
- **Email**: Instantly or Smartlead. Set up multiple sending domains (NEVER your main company domain — burn risk). Each domain gets 2–4 inboxes warmed for ~3 weeks before sending real volume. Plan for 30–50 emails/day per inbox max.
- **DMs**: manual at start, SOP-driven. Tools like InstaSafe / LinkedIn Helper exist but high ban risk — start manual.
- **Phone**: Aircall, Justcall, or similar. Local number for the niche.

## Sequencing rules

### Primary / Secondary / Tertiary
- **Primary** = your highest-volume, most-systematized channel. ~70% of activity. Don't add a secondary until primary is metrically proven (positive reply rate >2%, booked rate >0.5%).
- **Secondary** = added once primary is producing predictably. ~25% of activity. Used for relationship-building or higher-tier prospects.
- **Tertiary** = highest-touch, highest-conversion channel for top-tier ICPs only. ~5%. Often manual phone calls to your dream-100 list.

### Cold calling priority
Add cold calling to the stack when:
- Your niche actually answers phones (test: try 50 calls to your ICP — what % connects?).
- Email/DM has a deliverability or scale ceiling (you've maxed warmed inboxes).
- Your average deal size justifies the per-touch labor (rough rule: $3K+ deal size).

If <2% of cold calls connect to a decision-maker in your niche, deprioritize.

## The mad-scientist Lollapalooza strategy

Once your single-channel outbound is producing, layer multiple stimuli on the same prospect across channels in a coordinated sequence:

```
Day 1: Cold email
Day 2: LinkedIn connection request (no message)
Day 4: Cold email follow-up
Day 5: LinkedIn DM referencing the email
Day 7: Cold call referencing email + LinkedIn
Day 10: Final email "breakup" message
```

The prospect sees you on three channels in ten days. The brain's pattern recognition reads this as "this person is everywhere — they must be relevant." Cognitive availability + repeated exposure + reciprocation if you've added value = lollapalooza on the booking decision.

This only works once each individual channel is dialed. Don't multi-channel until single-channel works.

## The metrics you actually watch

Targets for a working B2B outbound (cold email):
- Deliverability: 95%+ inbox (not spam)
- Open rate: 40–70%
- Reply rate: 3–10%
- Positive reply rate: 1–3%
- Booked rate: 0.5–2% of total sent
- Show rate: 70–85% of booked
- Close rate (call → client): 25–50% (governed by `imperium-conversion-playbook`)

If any number falls below the floor, the bottleneck is at *that step*. Fix the bottleneck, not the next step. Most beginners increase send volume to fix a 0.5% reply rate. That's wrong — fix the stimulus.

## Common pitfalls

- **Skipping foundations.** Outbound on top of bad niche/offer = no calls. Audit `niche-selection-grade-a`, `offer-architecture`, `imperium-conversion-playbook` first.
- **Sending from the main company domain.** One spam complaint and your real email goes dark for weeks. Always use alt domains.
- **Not warming inboxes.** New inbox sending 50 cold emails on day 1 = blacklisted. Warm 3 weeks minimum.
- **Vanity volume.** "I sent 1000 emails today!" with no metric tracking = activity, not system.
- **Multi-channel before single-channel works.** You're hiding the bottleneck under noise.
- **Manual scheduling.** "Book a call by replying with times." Friction kills conversion. Use cal.com link in every message.
- **Same script forever.** Markets saturate. Refresh stimuli every 30–60 days minimum, more often if reply rate decays.
- **No follow-ups.** ~70% of replies come on follow-ups 2–6, not initial. The fortune is in the follow-up.
- **Confusing "no" with "not now."** Most "no" replies are just "not the right week." Keep them in the CRM and re-engage in 90 days.
- **Over-automating early.** You need to read the replies to feel the niche's pulse. Automate after you've manually handled 200+ replies.
- **Treating outbound as broken when sales is broken.** If you book calls but don't close, outbound is fine — `sales-bedrock` is broken. Diagnose accurately.

## Build sequence summary

1. Foundations check (niche, offer, identity, sales process). Do not proceed if any of the four are weak.
2. Scheduler (cal.com) configured.
3. Pick primary channel (default: cold email).
4. Stand up sending infrastructure (alt domains, warmed inboxes).
5. Build stimuli library using `asymmetric-psychological-leverage`.
6. Source 500–2,000 prospect lead list.
7. CRM sheet + metric tracker live.
8. Send first 100 messages. Read every reply. Tune.
9. Hit baseline metrics (1%+ positive reply, 0.5%+ booked).
10. Add follow-up sequence (4–7 touches).
11. Layer secondary channel (DM or phone).
12. Scale primary channel volume.
13. Stack lollapalooza multi-touch sequences once single-channel is proven.
