# Business Set-Up

> "The niche, the offer, the problem, the solution validation — those are the core foundation. This is just the dressing to seem legitimate."

## When to use

Load this skill any time the work involves:
- Naming the business and registering the domain
- Setting up a business email and the basic software stack
- Generating legal templates (service agreement, NDA, etc.)
- Designing a logo and a one-page website
- Setting up a professional LinkedIn
- Registering a legal entity (LLC, Ltd, etc.)
- Opening a business bank account and payment processor
- Auditing whether someone is procrastinating on the *real* foundations by perfecting the dressing

The 11 steps in this skill are deliberately *fast*. They should take 1–3 days end to end, not weeks. Time spent here over 1 week is time stolen from niche/offer/sales work that actually moves money.

---

## The 11 steps

### 1. Name the company
Set a 10-minute timer. Generate names. Check namecheap.com for domain availability. Pick one and move on. Do not consult a branding agency. Names do not move money — execution does.

Rules:
- 1–3 words.
- Pronounceable.
- .com available (or .ai, .co as backup).
- Not a tongue-twister, not a pun, not a reference to a movie.
- Niche-relevant or generic-professional. Avoid being too cute or too clever.

### 2. Register the domain
Buy on Namecheap or Cloudflare. Add WHOIS privacy. Cost: ~$10–15/year. Done.

### 3. Set up a business email
Google Workspace — `your.name@yourcompany.com`. Cost: ~$6/month. Required for cal.com, every legitimate sending platform, and basic professionalism. Do not use Gmail/Hotmail/personal addresses for B2B sales.

### 4. Generate legal documents and templates
Use ChatGPT or a lawyer (lawyer is better but not essential at start) to generate:
- Master Services Agreement (MSA) / Statement of Work (SOW)
- Mutual NDA
- Privacy policy + terms of service for the website
- Refund / cancellation policy

Templates are sufficient at zero revenue. Once you cross ~$10K MRR, hire a lawyer to formalize.

### 5. Design a logo
Use Canva, Midjourney, or Looka. Spend ≤2 hours. Ship one logo. Do not iterate to perfection. The logo will be replaced when the business is real. Pretending it's important now is procrastination.

### 6. Set up a one-page website
Use Carrd, Framer, or a Webflow template. Single page, single CTA (`Book a Call` to your cal.com link). Includes:
- Headline (niche + outcome)
- 2–3 sentence sub-headline
- 1–3 case studies / testimonials (placeholder until you have real ones)
- Calendar embed or Book a Call button
- Footer with company name, email, year

This page is *the only website you need* to close $50K+ in deals. Multi-page websites with blogs and "About" pages are not what closes the deal.

### 7. Set up a professional LinkedIn
- Headshot (real photo, not avatar).
- Headline: niche + outcome.
- About section: 200–400 words, first-person, niche-specific.
- Featured: link to the website + 1–2 case studies if you have them.
- 50+ connections in your niche to start the algorithm warming.

### 8. Register the legal entity
Country-dependent. General rules:
- US: LLC in Delaware, Wyoming, or your home state. Cost: ~$50–500.
- UK: Ltd company. Cost: ~£12.
- Outside US/UK: try to register a US LLC or UK Ltd if your country allows it. Operating from a third country with a US/UK entity simplifies banking and looks more legitimate to clients in those markets.

Hire an accountant/lawyer for ~$200–1,000. Worth it. Do not DIY tax structure.

### 9. Set up business banking
- US: Mercury, Relay, or local bank.
- UK: Revolut Business, Wise Business, or local bank.
- Other: depends on the entity. Mercury and Wise both onboard non-US founders for US LLCs.

Keep business and personal accounts strictly separate from day one. Mixing them is a tax nightmare and a credibility killer (clients can tell).

### 10. Set up payment processing
- Stripe is the default for most jurisdictions and currencies.
- Add a payment link or invoice template you can send within 30 seconds of closing a call.
- Test with a $1 transaction to yourself to confirm flow.

For high-ticket consulting, you can also accept wire transfers — sometimes preferred by enterprise clients. Stripe + wire covers 99% of cases.

### 11. Set up the software stack
Minimum stack:
- Cal.com (scheduling)
- Google Workspace (email + Drive)
- Stripe (payments)
- Notion or Google Docs (SOPs, contracts, internal knowledge)
- A CRM spreadsheet (Google Sheets) — upgrade to HubSpot/Pipedrive later
- Loom (recorded walk-throughs for clients/teammates)
- Slack or WhatsApp (client comms — defer until first client)

Total cost: ~$50–80/month. Do not buy ten more SaaS tools "to get organized." Tool sprawl is its own form of procrastination.

## The procrastination trap

Business setup is the form of work that *feels productive* without producing revenue. Beginners can spend three weeks here. Three weeks of perfect logos and software trials and a fancy website do not change the fact that the niche is wrong, the offer is vague, or the operator hasn't made a single sales call.

Hard rule: **All 11 steps in ≤3 working days.** If a step takes longer than its budget, ship the worst-acceptable version and return to it later.

The business gets real on the first paid call. Everything before that is dressing.

---

## What to skip until later

- Custom branded merchandise.
- Multi-page website.
- Blog.
- Email newsletter / marketing automation.
- Multiple email signatures.
- Logo iterations beyond v1.
- "Pitch deck" beyond a 1-pager.
- Custom domain email signatures with banners.
- Trademark registration.
- Multiple payment currencies / multi-region setup.

These all become important. None of them are important *before* revenue exists.
