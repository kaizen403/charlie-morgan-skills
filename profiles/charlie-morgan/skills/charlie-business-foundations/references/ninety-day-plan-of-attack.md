# The 90-Day Plan Of Attack

> "The next 90 days will determine whether you make it or not. You are at your maximum emotional high right now. Ride that wave. Lock in. Do nothing else but outreach and content."

## When to use

Load this skill any time someone is planning their first 90 days of client acquisition, designing a sprint, building accountability systems for outreach, or asking "what should I focus on right now?" This skill is also the right anchor when an operator is procrastinating, doing fake work (tweaking websites, hanging out in forums, watching more training), or has lost momentum.

Sister skills: `unrelenting-discipline-trial-by-fire` and `iterative-renaissance` are inseparable from this — Charlie himself instructs operators to re-watch them weekly during the 90-day sprint. Also load `building-outbound-systems` and `building-inbound-systems` for the actual mechanics.

## The Core Premise

You have just finished building your business infrastructure: niche, offer, methodology, sales call, outreach systems. Now there is one job and only one job: **put traffic through the system**. Everything you have built is worth nothing if you do not now do the work. Charlie's exact frame: "There is nothing else in your business that you need to do to grow. Nothing else. This is the most important thing."

The 90-day plan exists for one reason: you are at your **maximum emotional high** right now. In one to two months, that wave crashes. Doubt creeps in. Pain avoidance returns. You will want to "switch niches", "redesign the website", "do another course." All of that is resistance. The plan is the rail you ride so that when the high collapses, you keep doing the work anyway.

---

## The 90-Day Rules (non-negotiable)

1. **All in, all out.** Every waking minute, 12 hours a day, 7 days a week. Outreach plus content. Nothing else.
2. **200 outreaches a day.** That was Charlie's exact number when he got his first client. Some days you'll hit 240, some days 139. Goal: stay as close to 200 as possible every single day.
3. **Content batched.** You don't need to make content every day. Batch one or two YouTube videos a week, batch tweets/short content. Tick the box for the week if you've shipped your content commitment.
4. **No alternate work.** No website tweaks. No reading forums. No watching more sales call YouTube videos. No "researching new niches." No podcasts about business. No fancy software. Only outreach plus content shipping.
5. **No skipping weekends.** "There's no point cold calling on Sunday because no one's in." Wrong. The point is the *habit*, not the response rate. Do it anyway.
6. **Track it daily.** Build a simple spreadsheet: 91 days down the side, columns for content done (yes/no) and outreach count (out of 200). Tick boxes. Do not lie to the spreadsheet.
7. **Re-watch Trial By Fire and Iterative Renaissance every week.** Schedule it. Saturday or Sunday morning. Do not skip. These two videos are the antidote to pain avoidance and chaotic experimentation, the only two reasons you will fail.
8. **Public commitment.** After your first week of consistency, post in your community: "Here's my week one — all 200s." This converts your accountability from internal to external.

## The Tracker (build this exact sheet)

Columns:
- Day number (1–91)
- Date
- Day of week
- Content done? (yes/no)
- Outreach count (target: 200)
- Outreach method mix (cold call, cold DM, cold email — proportions)
- Notes (bookings, replies, lessons)

Rules for using the sheet:
- Fill it in **end of every day**, not at week-end.
- Never fill in a future day, never fudge past days.
- If you missed 200, write the real number. Honesty is the whole point.
- Post a photo of the sheet weekly to your community for accountability.

## Emotional Management — The Most Important Section

Right now, you are flying. You are excited, motivated, full of expectation. Big dreams. Big plans. Imagining the income.

This is the trap.

Because your **emotional altitude** is so high, any small obstacle (a bad sales call, a bad week of bookings, your methodology failing on the first client, a refund) will register on a deeply neurotic level. The crash is proportional to the high. About day 30–60, you are going to want to quit.

**The fix is to detach your emotions from outcomes and re-attach them to inputs.**

You cannot control:
- When your first client comes
- Whether the sales call closes
- Whether outreach replies come
- Market timing

You can control:
- Whether you sent 200 today
- Whether you shipped your content
- Whether you re-watched Trial By Fire this week
- Whether you stayed off forums and YouTube during work hours

> "Put all of your emotional attachment onto whether or not you're doing the work, instead of whether the work is working."

This is the central emotional discipline of the 90-day sprint. Until you internalize it, you will keep oscillating between euphoria and despair, neither of which moves you forward.

## Common Failure Modes (and the antidote)

### "I want to switch my niche"
This is week-3 doubt talking. Iterative renaissance: do not change the variable when you have not yet held it long enough to test it. Niche stays. Iterate the offer or the outreach script first.

### "My website / branding / pitch deck needs work"
This is fake work. None of those things book appointments. Outreach books appointments. If your existing assets are passable, leave them alone for 90 days.

### "I'll do 100 outreaches today, I have meetings"
Meetings are part of the 200 if they're sales calls. Otherwise the 200 still stands. The whole point is that the volume forces you to operate efficiently.

### "Sundays don't count"
They count. If only because they prove to you that you can do them.

### "I haven't booked any appointments in 3 weeks, so this isn't working"
Three weeks at 200/day is 4,200 touches. If 4,200 touches yields zero appointments, your *script or offer* needs iteration, not your effort. Use Iterative Renaissance methodology to change one variable at a time. Do not abandon. Iterate.

### "I'm exhausted"
You are not exhausted from the work. You are exhausted from the *resistance*. Trial By Fire applies. Pain avoidance is the enemy. The work itself, once you start, expands you. Rest is recovery, not avoidance.

## What 90 Days Actually Looks Like (Charlie's reality)

- Booked ~60 sales calls across 90 days.
- First client landed on day 88 or 89.
- Most calls in the early weeks went badly because sales skill was weak.
- The work was the iteration loop: do outreach, get on calls, learn from calls, refine script, refine offer, refine targeting.
- The result was not a smooth ramp — it was 88 days of low-signal grinding followed by one breakthrough.

Set your expectation accordingly. **You may not see results until day 60+.** That is normal. Anyone telling you otherwise is selling you something.

## The Pact (write this down on paper)

> For the next 90 days I commit to:
> - 200 outreaches a day, every day, including weekends
> - Shipping content on schedule
> - Re-watching Trial By Fire and Iterative Renaissance weekly
> - Detaching from outcomes and attaching to inputs
> - Posting weekly progress publicly
> - Doing nothing else with my work hours

> Signed: _____________  Date: _____________

This sounds corny. Charlie's whole point is that the people who do it actually do it, and the people who don't sign it don't actually do it.

## After 90 Days

Three outcomes are possible:
1. **You closed clients and built momentum.** Now you graduate into systems, hiring, and removing yourself from the outreach motion.
2. **You did the work and didn't close.** Iterate the script, the offer, or the targeting. You do not change effort — you change variables.
3. **You didn't do the work.** That is the real signal. The plan was never about the spreadsheet — the spreadsheet revealed whether you would actually do the thing. If you couldn't, no other plan will save you. Honest reckoning required.
