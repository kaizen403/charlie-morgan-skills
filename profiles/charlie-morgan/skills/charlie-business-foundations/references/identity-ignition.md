# Identity Ignition

> "You have to create a construct that can't fail." — Charlie Morgan, *2.3 Identity Ignition*

Behavior follows identity. You will revert to who you believe you are. If you believe you're "someone who can't sell," you will not sell, no matter how good your script is. The work is to install a new identity *first*, then let behavior follow.

## When to use this skill

- "I can't seem to follow through."
- "I know what I should do but I'm not doing it."
- "I feel like an imposter."
- "I'm scared to charge that much."
- "I don't think people will take me seriously."
- "I keep self-sabotaging."
- "I procrastinate even on things I want."
- "I can't see myself as the kind of person who [X]."

If a user is trying to change behavior without addressing identity, redirect here.

## The model

> "You behave as the person you believe you are. Change the belief about who you are, and behavior follows. Try to change behavior without changing identity, and behavior reverts."

This is the central failure mode of self-help: trying to bolt new behavior onto an old identity. The graft gets rejected. Within weeks, the person reverts.

The work order is:

```
1. Identify the identity you'd need to hold to make the new behavior natural.
2. Install that identity (specific practices below).
3. Let behavior follow as a consequence.
```

Not:
```
1. Force new behavior.
2. Hope identity catches up.
```

## How to install an identity

### Step 1 — Name the identity precisely
Vague identities don't install. "Successful person" is not an identity. Specificity:

- "I am a person who books 5 sales calls per week."
- "I am someone who charges premium prices and walks away from prospects who don't qualify."
- "I am an expert in [niche]'s [problem]."
- "I am someone who does the hard call before the easy task."

The narrower and more behaviorally-specific the identity, the easier to install.

### Step 2 — Audit your current identity
What does your current behavior reveal about who you currently believe you are? Don't lie to yourself.

- "I haven't made a cold call in 3 weeks." → current identity: someone who avoids cold calls.
- "I dropped my price three times this month." → current identity: someone whose work isn't worth the asking price.
- "I haven't done my action items." → current identity: a consumer of training, not an applier.

The current identity is whatever your behavior over the last 30 days reveals. Be honest.

### Step 3 — Install through action, not affirmation
Identity is not installed by reciting affirmations in the mirror. Identity is installed by acting *as* the new identity *before you feel ready*. Charlie's framing:

> "Create as if you are."

Specifically:
- Charge the price the new identity charges. Even on the first call.
- Make the calls the new identity makes. Even when scared.
- Walk away from the deals the new identity walks away from. Even when cash is tight.

Each act of acting-as-if rewires the identity. Each act of avoidance reinforces the old one.

### Step 4 — Environment + inputs
Identity is also reinforced (or sabotaged) by environment:

- Who you spend time with (do they share or contradict the new identity?)
- What you consume (content that affirms or undermines the identity?)
- What you wear, say, write (small acts of self-presentation matter more than people think)
- The rooms you put yourself in (paid masterminds, communities, conferences with peers in the new identity)

You can't install "I'm a $20k/mo consultant" while spending 12 hours/day in environments that reflect "I'm an unemployed person trying to figure things out."

### Step 5 — Tolerate the discomfort of the gap
Between the old identity and the new one is a period where you feel like a fraud. This is not a sign you're doing it wrong. This is the gap.

> "You're going to feel like an imposter. That's because you are. You're impersonating the new identity until your behavior catches up and makes it real."

Push through. The feeling fades. The identity locks in.

## Failure modes

- **Trying to change behavior without identity.** Revert within weeks.
- **Identity is too vague.** "Be more confident" doesn't install. "I am someone who states my price without flinching" installs.
- **Affirmations without action.** Saying it doesn't make it real. Acting as if does.
- **Bad environment.** Trying to install a new identity in environments that reflect the old one. Either change the environment or accept the new identity will be slow to set.
- **Quitting in the gap.** The fraud feeling is the work, not a sign to stop.

## Action items

1. Write down the identity you'd need to hold to make your business work. Be behaviorally specific. (One sentence.)
2. List 3 actions in the next 7 days that the new identity would naturally take. Schedule them.
3. List 1 input in your environment (a person, a feed, a habit) that contradicts the new identity. Remove or reduce it this week.
