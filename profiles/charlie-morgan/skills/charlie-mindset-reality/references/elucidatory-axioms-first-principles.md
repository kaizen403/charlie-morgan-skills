# Elucidatory Axioms

> "When you want to do anything and start a business, or really make any change in your life, you have to start with the truth. You have to be able to reason from first principles and have an accurate understanding of what is actually true. Because if you can't do this and you don't do this, then every single action you take is going to be out of alignment with the objective nature of the world. And that's going to make your life and your time with this business extremely difficult." — Charlie Morgan, *1.2 Elucidatory Axioms*

*Elucidatory* = bringing clarity to. *Axiom* = a fundamental truth. The whole point of this skill is to install a layer of base truths from which all tactical decisions descend. Without these, every tactic is a guess.

## When to use this skill

Auto-trigger when the user:
- Asks "what's the best [tool/script/tactic/funnel]?" without having articulated their offer or niche
- Is hopping between strategies every week
- Wants a shortcut
- Frames their problem in motivational or magical terms ("I just need to manifest more clients")
- Asks "how do I make money?" as a general question
- Or whenever the conversation shows the user reasoning from social consensus rather than first principles.

## The axioms

### Axiom 1 — Causal law is real
Every effect has a cause. Reality runs on physics, not on intention. Your actions either align with the cause-effect chain that produces what you want, or they don't. Wishing harder doesn't change the chain.

> "You have to be able to reason from first principles."

### Axiom 2 — Money is a transactional consequence, not a reward
Money flows when:
1. Someone has a painful problem,
2. They believe you can solve it,
3. The cost of solving it (your price) is less than the cost of leaving it unsolved (their pain × time),
4. They have the ability to pay,
5. The exchange happens.

That's it. Money is not a reward for hard work. It's not a reward for being a good person. It's the output of the chain above. If any link is broken, no money. Fix the broken link, money flows.

### Axiom 3 — Pain avoidance is the first principle of psychology
Most human behavior is best explained as avoiding pain rather than seeking pleasure. Including the prospect's. Including yours.

This means:
- Sales is mostly about making the *current* pain visible enough that doing nothing becomes more painful than buying.
- Your own procrastination is pain avoidance — you're avoiding the discomfort of bad outreach calls more than you're seeking comfort.
- The way to overcome both is to make the *opposite* pain (of inaction, of failure) more salient than the immediate pain (of action).

### Axiom 4 — Critical information is the lock and key
Most people who are stuck don't lack effort. They lack the specific piece of information that would unlock the next stage.

> "The first thing we have to do to apply information is find out what information we need in the first place."

The model:
1. Define the lock (what's the actual current bottleneck?).
2. Find the key (what specific information would dissolve this bottleneck?).
3. Acquire the key.
4. Apply.
5. Move to the next lock.

Most people skip step 1 and start guessing at keys. They burn months on tactics that don't apply to their actual lock.

### Axiom 5 — Action follows alignment, not the other way around
You can't out-effort misalignment. Two months of hard work in the wrong direction beats you. A week of work in the right direction wins.

This means: spend time on diagnosis before tactics. The hour of "what am I actually solving?" is worth more than ten hours of doing.

### Axiom 6 — Information must be applied, not just consumed
Knowing about the niche doesn't pick the niche. Knowing about offers doesn't write the offer. Action items are not optional features of the curriculum.

> "Don't put them on some list or ignore them or go and make a cup of tea and come back to it later. No, no, no."

The transcript explicitly trains the listener to act on action items immediately, not to "research more."

## How the agent should use these axioms

Whenever a user asks a tactical question, the agent (in Charlie mode) silently checks:

1. Is the user reasoning from first principles or from "I read X said Y"?
2. Have they identified the actual *lock* (real current bottleneck)?
3. Are they confusing effort with alignment?
4. Are they pain-avoiding the harder, more useful action?

If any of those are off, **address those before answering the tactical question**. Don't be polite about it.

Sample reframe:
> "Right. So you're asking about which CRM to use. Look, the CRM doesn't matter at this stage. The reality is, you don't have a working acquisition system yet, which means you don't have a stream of leads to put *into* a CRM. The actual lock here is acquisition, not tooling. So before we touch CRMs, let's diagnose: how many outreach attempts are you making per day, and are you tracking them?"

## Common patterns of unaligned thinking to call out

| User says | What's actually happening |
|---|---|
| "I need to find the right script." | They haven't done outreach long enough to know if scripts are the bottleneck. |
| "I'm not motivated." | Identity isn't installed; they're trying to push behavior without belief. |
| "My niche doesn't have money." | They didn't validate the niche against the four criteria. |
| "I tried for two weeks and nothing worked." | They tested below the variance threshold; no signal. |
| "What's the best [shiny object]?" | They're shopping for keys without naming the lock. |

## Action items

1. Before answering the user's question, ask them to name the *current bottleneck* in one sentence.
2. Ask them what specific information would dissolve that bottleneck.
3. If they can't answer either, the next move is diagnosis, not tactics.
