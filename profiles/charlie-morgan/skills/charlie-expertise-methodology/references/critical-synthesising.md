# Critical Synthesising — How Consultants Manufacture Expertise

> "How do you take a problem that you know nothing about and have no experience solving, learn how to solve it, and not only just learn how to solve it in theory, but go out and do it in practice and get so good at it that you can charge thousands of dollars to people to do this? It's actually really simple."
> — Charlie Morgan

## When to use this skill

Load this skill any time the user is staring at the gap between **"I picked a niche / I see the problem"** and **"I can deliver a result a client will pay thousands for."** This is the bridge between niche selection and actually charging money. It is the master mental model that frames the entire week-3 sequence — every other week-3 skill (`defining-problem`, `critical-information-overload`, `mapping-methodology`, `iterative-expertise`, `guaranteeing-results`, `second-order-problems`) is a phase of synthesising.

Use this skill for:
- Consultants/agency owners who don't yet have a methodology
- People paralysed because they "don't know enough" to deliver
- Anyone who thinks they need 10 years of experience before charging
- Framing the journey from chaos → ordered methodology

## The first principle: cause → contact → effect

Synthesising is named after Hegel's thesis/antithesis/synthesis. Every result in business is a *synthesis* produced by bringing a cause into contact with a subject:

- **Thesis** = your niche client *with* the problem (current state)
- **Antithesis** = you, your methodology, your applied information (the cause)
- **Contact** = your delivery mechanism (DFY / DWY / DIY)
- **Synthesis** = the client *without* the problem (effect, value added, money earned)
- **Latency** = time between contact and synthesis

> "We go from cause to effect by contact. And time is a delay between a cause happening and an effect manifesting."

A consultant is a professional synthesiser. You take chaos (a problem, scattered information, a confused client) and produce ordered effect (the result they wanted).

## The synthesiser's six-step process

This is the macro framework. Each step has a dedicated week-3 skill — load them in order.

### 1. Define the problem ruthlessly
Without a precisely defined problem, every downstream step is corrupted. Charlie: *"If your foundations are off, then the house will crumble."* → Load `defining-problem`.

### 2. Information overload
Drown your brain in every piece of media on the problem for 3–4 days, 12–16 hours/day. Quantity over quality. Treat yourself like an LLM hungry for training data. → Load `critical-information-overload`.

### 3. Map your methodology (MVM)
Take the chaos, untangle it, sequence it into the **Minimum Viable Methodology**: a linear chain of *critical, objective, action-based* steps. → Load `mapping-methodology`.

### 4. Iterate scientifically
Your version 1 will be wrong. Sign 3–5 clients, observe, iterate via the scientific method. Methodology improvement is *never* finished. → Load `iterative-expertise`.

### 5. Guarantee the result
Money-back guarantee tied to outcome + timeframe + risk reversal. Holds you accountable, builds prospect confidence, forces methodology improvement. → Load `guaranteeing-results`.

### 6. Stack second-order problems
Once you reliably solve problem #1, the act of solving it surfaces problem #2 — sell that next. → Load `second-order-problems`.

## Mental models that make synthesising work

### You are not original — you are a remixer
> "Be prepared to copy, beg, borrow, steal, and rip off. Your first methodology hypothesis is gonna be a Frankenstein thing put together by other peoples' expertise, ideas, and methods. And there's no shame."

Pride is the enemy. The fastest path to a working methodology is shameless copying of competitors. Originality is for after you have 100+ clients.

### Order from chaos
Imagine wired headphones tangled in a backpack. That's your information overload. Synthesising = patiently untangling them into a linear sequence someone can follow.

### Done-for-you / Done-with-you / Do-it-yourself
All three contain the word "DONE." Information without action is theory; theory without contact is worthless. The client *or* you *or* both must execute.

## Common failure modes

- **Trying to be original too early.** You haven't earned the right. Copy first, innovate at $1M+.
- **Skipping problem definition.** Building a beautiful solution to the wrong problem.
- **Theory-crafting forever.** Information gathering becomes a hiding place from sales calls.
- **Falling in love with v1.** Refusing to iterate because you're emotionally attached.
- **Believing you must "feel ready."** You won't. Sign clients while still uncomfortable.
- **Confusing "I have read about it" with "I have done it."** A boxer who watched 100 hours of footage still gets knocked out in round one.

## Action items

1. Print the six-step list above. Tape it where you work.
2. Identify which step you are currently on. Be honest.
3. If step 1 is incomplete, stop everything and load `defining-problem`.
4. Set a 3–4 day calendar block within the next 7 days for full information overload.
5. Identify 3–5 competitors who already solve this problem. Add their names to a doc — these are your synthesis source material.
6. Accept emotionally that **your first methodology will be wrong**, and that is the correct outcome.
