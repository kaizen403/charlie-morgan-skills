# Sales Bedrock

> "Sales is fucking easy. Now, that's easy for me to say, because I've done a lot of sales calls. But if you believe sales is easy, then over the course of a few months and enough calls, sales will become very easy for you." — Charlie Morgan, *4.2 Sales Bedrock*

Strategies sit on top of beliefs. If your beliefs about sales are broken, no script will save you. This skill is the bedrock layer underneath `imperium-conversion-playbook` and `conquering-objections-vault`. Load it first.

## When to use this skill

## Why bedrock matters

Charlie's analogy: trying to sell with a script but no foundations is like learning French from a phrasebook without learning that nouns have gender. You'll say words. None of it will land. Prospects will look at you like "what the hell is this guy on about?"

> "There's loads of salespeople out there earning multi-six and seven-figure incomes. And there's loads of salespeople out there that don't have any income at all. What's the one thing that separates them? It's not the script they're using, I can tell you that. It's their beliefs around sales."

The bedrock is a small set of beliefs you have to install *before* the script will work.

## The 6 bedrock beliefs

### 1. Sales is easy

Not because it requires no skill — it requires lots — but because believing it's hard creates a confirmation-bias feedback loop that decimates you.

> "If you come in thinking it's hard, then all you're gonna do is start to look for evidence that sales is hard. It's called the confirmation bias. And the feedback loops that revolve around that thing will just decimate any hope you have of making any money."

Drop the belief at the door. Tell yourself sales is easy. After enough reps, it becomes true.

### 2. Sales is helping people, not extracting from them

This is the single most leverage-producing belief shift. If you believe sales is "convincing someone to give you money," every call will feel sleazy, you'll soften on objections to be liked, and you'll lose deals you should win. If you genuinely believe you can help them and that *not* selling them is *worse* for them, your posture changes entirely.

> "Sales is about helping people. Let go of any attachment you have to your conversion rates, your commissions, or your paychecks. Put all of your attention and attachment onto genuinely helping people, and all the good stuff will come naturally. Good conversion rates, high commissions, and making money is all just a consequence of genuinely helping people."

The line Charlie uses, said with full sincerity:

> "If I don't get you on board here, I'm not going to be able to sleep tonight, because I genuinely think I can help you. And for me to not tell you that, and for me to just give up, would be an awful thing to do, because I can help you."

That line only works if you actually mean it. Build offers you actually believe in. Then sell hard.

### 3. The prospect's problems are real and ugly — your job is to make them visible

Most prospects walk into a call vaguely aware they have a problem but unable to articulate how bad it is. The first part of the call is *not* selling — it's diagnosis. You make their current situation specific, you get metrics, data points, costs, timelines, and you let them feel the pain of where they actually are.

> "Most business owners… are kind of unaware to the fact that their situation and their reality can be improved. Before you can understand whether or not you can improve something, you have to sort of understand where you're at. Usually, just by doing this, it will create a lot of pain, because people will start to realize that their current situation isn't all that good."

Pain is not something you inflict. Pain is something you reveal. It was already there.

### 4. The buyer is driven by pain → desire → confidence, not features

The mental model of the buyer:

```
Current State (with pain) ──→ Desired State (with relief / outcome)
                              ↑
                    Mechanism (how you bridge it)
                              ↑
                    Confidence (that mechanism actually works for them)
```

A sale happens when:
- Pain in the current state is **vivid**
- The desired state is **specific** (not "more revenue" but "$50k/mo with predictable lead flow")
- The mechanism between them is **credible**
- Confidence in you is **high enough** that risk feels acceptable

If any one of these is weak, no close technique will save the call.

### 5. Posture: you are the doctor, not the supplicant

You are not begging them to buy. You are diagnosing whether they're a fit. If they're not, you tell them so. If they are, you tell them what's required. The frame is doctor/patient, not vendor/customer.

Practical tells you've got the wrong posture:
- You discount unprompted.
- You apologize for the price.
- You let them set the agenda.
- You "follow up" when they ghost.
- You change your offer mid-call to match their objection.

Practical tells you've got the right posture:
- You set the agenda.
- You hold price until they ask.
- You disqualify out loud when it's not a fit.
- You name the truth even when it's uncomfortable.
- You don't chase.

### 6. You will be cringy at first. Do it anyway.

> "My first sales calls were hilariously cringy. I've done over a thousand sales calls at this point, and the only reason I get the results I do is because I stuck it out and didn't stop doing them. If I went back and listened to my first couple of sales calls, I would cringe to the core of my existence."

The competence threshold for sales is high but reachable. The only people who don't get there are the ones who quit at call 4, 12, or 25. See `oscillations-of-doubt` for why.

## The mental model of a buyer

Every prospect on a call is some mix of:

- **Pain** ↑ → action threshold ↓ (they buy faster)
- **Comfort** ↑ → action threshold ↑ (they stall)
- **Desire** ↑ → action threshold ↓
- **Indifference** ↑ → action threshold ↑
- **Confidence in you** ↑ → action threshold ↓
- **Doubt** ↑ → action threshold ↑

Your only job on the call is to push the first column up and the second column down until you cross their personal action threshold. That's it. Everything in `imperium-conversion-playbook` is just structured ways to do that.

Some prospects have low action thresholds — they buy in 20 minutes. Others need 60+ minutes of careful work. **Their motivation is your responsibility.** Don't blame "unmotivated prospects" — blame your inability to find what already motivates them.

## Identity required

Before the call, the identity you're walking in with should sound like:

- "I have something genuinely good. They need it. My job is to figure out if they're a fit and, if they are, to make sure they don't walk away from this because of fear."
- "I'm not begging. I'm offering."
- "If they say no, that's data, not damage."
- "Whatever they object with, I have a clear answer. I've heard it before."

If your inner monologue before a call sounds like "please please please buy please" — close the laptop and re-read this skill before dialing.

## Do / Don't

**Do**
- Believe sales is easy before you have evidence it is.
- Believe your offer genuinely helps. (If you don't, fix the offer, not the script.)
- Treat the call as a diagnosis, not a pitch.
- Hold posture even when uncomfortable.
- Embrace the cringy reps early.

**Don't**
- Don't sell something you wouldn't buy.
- Don't soften on price to be liked.
- Don't chase. Don't follow up forever.
- Don't blame the prospect's "motivation."
- Don't change strategies based on 4 calls of evidence (see `oscillations-of-doubt`).

## Action items

1. Write down your top 5 current beliefs about sales. Cross out the ones that are versions of "sales is hard" or "sales is sleazy." Replace each with the bedrock equivalent.
2. Write a 30-second answer to: "Why is your offer genuinely helpful to the right buyer?" If you can't answer it cleanly, fix the offer.
3. Write your pre-call ritual — the 60 seconds before you dial. What posture are you reminding yourself of?
4. Commit to 30 calls before you change anything (see `oscillations-of-doubt` and `script-modification`).
5. Then load `imperium-conversion-playbook` and run the structure on top of this foundation.
