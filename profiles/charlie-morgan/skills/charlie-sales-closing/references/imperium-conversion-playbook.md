# Imperium Conversion Playbook

> "Sales is belief transfer." — Charlie Morgan, *4.3 Imperium Conversion Playbook*

A high-ticket sales call is not "convincing" the prospect. It is making the prospect's existing pain visible to them, presenting a credible mechanism to remove it, and removing the risk so the rational choice is to buy. If you do this well, the close is a formality.

## When to use this skill

- "Walk me through a sales call."
- "How do I close more?"
- "What should I say at [point in the call]?"
- "I keep losing deals at [point]."
- "I'm getting on calls but not closing."
- "How do I price-anchor / set the agenda / handle the partner objection?"

For *specific* objection handling, also load `conquering-objections-vault`.
For emotional regulation through losing streaks, also load `oscillations-of-doubt`.

## The shape of the call

Source: `week4/4_2__sales_bedrock.txt`, `week4/4_3__imperium_conversion_playbook.txt`. Roughly:

```
1. Rapport (90 seconds, not more)
2. Agenda set
3. Discovery (the painful problem, current state, costs of staying stuck)
4. Mechanism (your process, how you solve it, why you specifically)
5. Pricing (only when they ask, never before)
6. Risk reversal / guarantee
7. Close
8. Objection handling (if any)
9. Onboarding scheduling
```

Each phase has a specific job. Skipping phases or doing them out of order kills the call.

## The 8 phases in detail

### 1. Rapport — fast, real, not a chat
Two minutes max. Brief friendly contact, then take control.

> "Don't spend twenty minutes chatting about their dog. They didn't book a call to chat. They booked because they have a problem."

### 2. Agenda — explicit, agreed
You set the agenda explicitly:

> "John, here's how I run these. I'm gonna ask you about [problem area] for the first 15-20 minutes to make sure I really understand where you are. Then if it makes sense, I'll walk you through how we'd help you get to [outcome]. At the end, if it's a fit, I'll tell you what it costs and we'll figure out if it makes sense to move forward. If it's not a fit, I'll tell you that too. Sound good?"

The agenda does three things:
- Pre-frames the close as "natural last step"
- Earns permission to ask hard discovery questions
- Anchors that price comes at the end (not the start)

If they don't agree to the agenda, the rest of the call is broken. Get the explicit yes.

### 3. Discovery — the painful problem, costs, urgency
This is where the deal is mostly won or lost. You are *not* selling here. You are diagnosing. Specifically, you make the prospect describe their own pain in their own words. The pain becomes more vivid to *them* as they articulate it.

Key questions:
- "Walk me through what's happening right now in [problem area]."
- "How long has this been going on?"
- "What have you tried so far?"
- "What's it costing you — in revenue, time, stress, opportunity — every month this stays unsolved?"
- "If this keeps going for the next 6 months, what does that look like?"
- "What would solving this fully be worth to you?"

> "The prospect's pain has to be more vivid than their fear of the price."

If they don't have pain, don't keep selling. Disqualify and move on.

### 4. Mechanism — credible, specific, not a feature dump
Once you understand the pain, you walk them through *how* you solve it. Not features. Mechanism.

The structure:
- "Here's what I see is happening: [restate problem in their words]"
- "Here's why most attempts fail: [common failure mode]"
- "Here's the mechanism we use: [3-5 named steps]"
- "Here's what happens at each step: [brief]"
- "Here's why this specifically works for [their niche/situation]"

The prospect must be able to picture the work happening. Vague selling = no buying.

### 5. Pricing — only when they ask, never before

> "Number one rule of price for us is that we don't tell them it until they ask us." — Charlie, *4.5 Conquering Objections*

If you give the price before they're convinced of the value, you get evaluated on the price alone. Hold it until they ask. They will ask if you've done the discovery and mechanism well.

Then deliver it cleanly:

> "It's $X. We typically work with clients for a minimum of [period]."

Don't apologize. Don't rush. Don't soften with a discount. Pause. Let them respond.

### 6. Risk reversal — explicit, named, comforting
Restate the guarantee in plain language.

> "If we don't [outcome] by [timeframe], here's what happens."

### 7. Close — direct ask, not slick
The close is one sentence:

> "Do you wanna get started?"

or

> "Does that make sense to move forward?"

That's it. No "limited time offer." No urgency manufactured out of nothing. The pain you've made vivid + the mechanism you've made credible + the risk you've removed = the rational choice is yes.

### 8. Objection handling
If the prospect raises objections, you handle them with the vault. Load `conquering-objections-vault`.

The five most common are:
- Price ("it's expensive" / "I can't afford it")
- Think about it ("I want to think about it")
- Partner ("I need to talk to my partner")
- Timing ("not right now" / "after [event]")
- Skepticism ("how do I know this'll work?")

Each has a specific rebuttal pattern.

## What separates closers from non-closers

> "The prospect has to have conviction that you can do what you say you can do."

You earn that conviction in *discovery* and *mechanism*. Not in clever closes. If your close-rate is bad, ~80% of the time the fix is in discovery (you're not making the pain vivid enough) or mechanism (you're not credible enough).

Common fixes:
- **Low close rate:** lengthen and deepen discovery. Make the pain real to *them*.
- **Lots of "think about it":** mechanism is too vague or the agenda wasn't set.
- **Price objection at the end:** you gave the price too early or didn't establish enough value.
- **Partner objection:** you didn't qualify decision-maker upfront.

## Action items

1. Write your sales call agenda script. Memorize it.
2. Write your top 10 discovery questions. Drill them.
3. Write your mechanism walkthrough as 3-5 named steps. Practice saying it in 90 seconds.
4. Write your one-sentence close. Practice the pause after delivering price.
5. Build the objection vault (load `conquering-objections-vault`).
